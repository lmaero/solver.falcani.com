# falcani: Content Strategy & Distribution Engine

> This document is the operational companion to the falcani Brand Guidelines. While the brand guide defines who falcani is, this document defines what falcani publishes, where it publishes, how often, and how to measure whether it's working. It is designed for both human content creators and AI systems generating content on falcani's behalf. Every piece of content produced using this strategy must pass the Anti-AI-Detection acid test defined in the brand guide.

---

## 1. Strategic Foundation

### What This Strategy Does

This document creates a system where every piece of falcani content has a clear purpose, a defined audience, a specific channel, and a measurable outcome. Without it, content defaults to generic patterns, formulaic structures, and the kind of AI-detectable output the brand guide explicitly bans. With it, every post, article, reel, and newsletter issue sounds like falcani and drives toward a conversation.

### Content Goals (Ranked by Priority)

1. **Lead generation.** Content should drive conversations with SME owners, operations heads, and finance leaders who are feeling the pain of broken systems. Every piece of content should make someone think "that's exactly my problem" and give them a clear path to talk to falcani.

2. **Brand recognition.** Build awareness of falcani as the custom software factory for SMEs. Not a digital agency, not a consulting firm, not a generic tech company. The specific position: "we build the software that replaces your broken processes."

3. **Trust building.** SME buyers are skeptical because they've been burned. Content should demonstrate competence through specifics (numbers, timelines, real scenarios), not through claims ("we're the best," "innovative solutions"). Show the work.

4. **Verb propagation.** Every content touchpoint is an opportunity for someone new to encounter #falcanize and feel the pull of the word. The verb spreads through experiences, not campaigns. Content should create moments that make the verb feel natural.

### Audience Map

The brand guide defines three personas: Linda (Owner/CEO), Marcus (Head of Operations), and Sophie (CFO/Finance Lead). Content doesn't target all three at once. Each piece speaks to one.

**Where they spend time online:**

Linda scrolls LinkedIn during her morning coffee and Instagram in the evening. She reads newsletters on Tuesday mornings. She doesn't search for technical content; she searches for "how to scale my business without losing control." She clicks on stories about other business owners who solved problems like hers.

Marcus lives in LinkedIn and technical blogs. He searches for specific solutions: "how to migrate from spreadsheets to a custom system," "ERP alternatives for mid-size companies." He reads documentation. He follows technical thought leaders. He doesn't care about brand stories; he cares about proof.

Sophie reads LinkedIn selectively and pays attention to ROI-focused content. She searches for "cost of manual processes," "software ROI calculator," and "total cost of ownership custom software." She clicks on anything with real numbers.

## 2. Content Pillars

Ten thematic pillars that organize all falcani content. Every piece of content must tie to at least one pillar. No content exists outside these pillars unless a new pillar is formally added.

### Pillar 1: The Cost of Doing Nothing

**Theme:** Quantifying the pain of broken processes, manual workflows, and disconnected systems.
**Primary persona:** Sophie (numbers), Linda (emotional weight)
**Content examples:** "Your team spends 120 hours a month on data entry. Here's what that costs." "The real price of staying on spreadsheets." Calculator tools showing cost-of-inaction.
**Channel fit:** LinkedIn (analytical), Blog (long-form breakdowns), Newsletter (deep dives with real math)

### Pillar 2: Before and After

**Theme:** Transformation stories showing what changed when falcani built something.
**Primary persona:** Linda (outcome), Marcus (technical detail)
**Content examples:** Case studies, side-by-side screenshots, "30 hours/month to zero" narratives, video walkthroughs of completed systems.
**Channel fit:** Instagram (visual before/after), LinkedIn (narrative case studies), Blog (detailed breakdowns)

### Pillar 3: How We Build

**Theme:** Pulling back the curtain on falcani's process, methodology, and decision-making.
**Primary persona:** Marcus (process validation), Linda (trust building)
**Content examples:** "Why we chose this architecture for a logistics client." "What our first week on a new project looks like." Design decision walkthroughs. Code-to-result demonstrations.
**Channel fit:** Blog (technical depth), LinkedIn (process highlights), Instagram (behind-the-scenes)

### Pillar 4: The Anti-Corporate Manifesto

**Theme:** Challenging corporate software industry norms, calling out what's broken about how most vendors operate.
**Primary persona:** Linda (validation of her frustrations), Marcus (solidarity)
**Content examples:** "Why your last software vendor failed you." "The meeting that should have been a demo." "Stop paying for PowerPoints about software." Hot takes on industry practices.
**Channel fit:** LinkedIn (thought leadership with teeth), Instagram (short punchy takes), Newsletter (longer rants)

### Pillar 5: Small Business Champions

**Theme:** Celebrating SMEs, their resilience, their problems, and why they deserve better technology.
**Primary persona:** Linda (identity), Sophie (business case)
**Content examples:** Stories about SME operators. The gap between enterprise tools and SME reality. Why the tech industry ignores the middle market. Client spotlights (with permission).
**Channel fit:** LinkedIn (storytelling), Instagram (client celebration), Newsletter (features)

### Pillar 6: The Solver Life

**Theme:** What it's like to work at falcani, how solvers think, the culture in practice.
**Primary persona:** All (different angle for each)
**Content examples:** Day-in-the-life content. How a solver approaches a new problem. The anti-hierarchy in action. Celebrating solver wins. Recruiting content that doubles as brand content.
**Channel fit:** Instagram (authentic, visual), LinkedIn (culture posts), Blog (longer reflections)

### Pillar 7: Technical Education

**Theme:** Teaching concepts that help SME decision-makers understand technology without needing to become technologists.
**Primary persona:** Marcus (deep), Linda (accessible), Sophie (ROI-adjacent)
**Content examples:** "What is an API and why should you care?" "The difference between custom software and SaaS, explained without jargon." "How to evaluate whether your business needs a custom system." Architecture decisions explained in business terms.
**Channel fit:** Blog (primary home), LinkedIn (condensed versions), Newsletter (curated picks)

### Pillar 8: The #falcanize Moment

**Theme:** Content specifically designed to showcase the verb in action and invite organic adoption.
**Primary persona:** All
**Content examples:** Client reaction videos (with permission). "Another system #falcanized" posts. The Pateti story and verb replication pattern. Compilation posts of client quotes.
**Channel fit:** Instagram (emotional, visual), LinkedIn (story format), Newsletter (celebration section)

### Pillar 9: Origin and Identity

**Theme:** The falcani story, the family name, the aerospace roots, the founder's journey, the philosophy behind the brand.
**Primary persona:** Linda (trust through authenticity), all (brand depth)
**Content examples:** The naming story. "Why an aeronautical engineer started a software company." The Zipaquirá walk. Values in practice, not just on paper.
**Channel fit:** Instagram (personal storytelling), LinkedIn (founder narrative), Blog (deep origin pieces)

### Pillar 10: The Uncomfortable Truth

**Theme:** Honest, potentially controversial takes on technology, business, and the software industry.
**Primary persona:** All (different hooks for each)
**Content examples:** "Most software projects fail because the client was sold a fantasy." "Your team doesn't resist change; they resist bad tools." "The consultant-to-coder pipeline is broken." "Why we sometimes tell clients not to build anything."
**Channel fit:** LinkedIn (high engagement potential), Newsletter (deeper exploration), Instagram (short provocations)

## 3. Channel Strategy

### Instagram (@falcani.software)

**Role:** Visual storytelling, brand personality, behind-the-scenes authenticity, Cian's primary stage.
**Audience:** Linda-adjacent (business owners who scroll visually), younger decision-makers, potential solvers.
**Language:** Bilingual. Default to the audience majority; for a new account, test both and track engagement by language.

**Content mix per week (3-4 posts):**
- 1 Reel: Before/after transformation or Cian explaining a concept (Pillars 2, 7, 8)
- 1 Carousel: Educational breakdown or process reveal (Pillars 3, 7, 10)
- 1 Story series: Behind-the-scenes, solver life, work in progress (Pillar 6)
- 1 Static or Reel: Anti-corporate take, origin story, or client celebration (Pillars 4, 5, 9)

**Visual rules:** Deep Navy backgrounds dominate. Cyan accents for emphasis. Outfit font for text overlays. No stock photography. No generic motivational quotes. Every image should look like it came from falcani's world, not from a template library.

**Bio:** "custom software factory for SMEs. simplify. optimize. falcanize. falcani.com | ineedasoftwarefor.com"

**Growth phases (for accounts under 1,000 followers):**
- First 30 days: Post daily to signal activity to the algorithm. Prioritize Reels (higher organic reach for new accounts). Content should be 70% educational (Pillars 7, 3), 20% personality (Pillars 6, 9), 10% direct transformation stories (Pillar 2).
- Days 31-90: Settle into 3-4x/week cadence. Start incorporating Cian. Engage with comments aggressively; every response is a brand touchpoint.
- Days 91+: Evaluate what's working. Double down on top-performing content types. Introduce the newsletter CTA in bio link.

### LinkedIn (falcani company page)

**Role:** Professional credibility, thought leadership with teeth, lead generation for Marcus and Sophie personas.
**Audience:** Marcus (technical validation), Sophie (ROI content), Linda (peer stories).
**Language:** English primary, Spanish when the content specifically addresses Latin American market or when the post's angle benefits from bilingual authenticity.

**Bio:** "custom software factory for small and medium businesses. simplify. optimize. falcanize."

**Content mix per week (2-3 posts):**
- 1 Long-form text post: Deep take on a Pillar 4 or 10 topic. No bullet points. Written as a story or argument, not a listicle. 800-1200 words. This is the high-engagement play.
- 1 Technical insight: Pillar 3 or 7 content condensed for LinkedIn. Can include a single image or diagram. Keep it under 600 words. End with a question, not a CTA.
- 1 Transformation or client story: Pillar 2 or 5 content. Numbers first, narrative second. If possible, include a client quote (with permission).

**What falcani LinkedIn content never does:**
- Checkmark bullet point lists
- Posts that start with a generic observation ("Many software projects fail because...")
- Emoji section dividers
- Formulaic structures: problem > checkmarks > CTA. Every post should feel different from the last.
- Posts that could appear on any tech company's page with the logo swapped

**What falcani LinkedIn content always does:**
- Opens with a specific, surprising detail ("A client's operations manager was spending every Friday afternoon rebuilding the same report from scratch. Fifty-two Fridays a year.")
- Writes in first person when the founder or a solver has a genuine story to tell
- Ends posts with a thought or question, not always a sales CTA
- Shares genuine mistakes and what falcani learned from them

### Blog (falcani.com)

**Role:** SEO, depth, technical credibility, evergreen content that drives organic traffic months after publication.
**Audience:** Marcus (primary, technical searches), Sophie (ROI searches), Linda (problem-aware searches).
**Language:** English primary. Create Spanish versions of top-performing posts, not all posts.

**Publishing cadence:** 2 posts per month minimum. Quality over quantity. Each post should be comprehensive enough that someone searching for the topic finds everything they need without clicking away.

**Content structure by type:**

**Tutorial posts (Pillar 7):** 1500-2500 words. Working code examples. Screenshots. Step-by-step without condescension. Title format: "How to [specific outcome] without [common pain point]." Example: "How to replace your Excel reporting with a custom dashboard without disrupting your team's workflow."

**Case study posts (Pillar 2):** 1000-1500 words. Structure: The problem (in the client's words), the approach (brief, not a process narrative), the result (numbers), the aftermath (what changed 3-6 months later). Include at least one direct client quote.

**Opinion posts (Pillars 4, 10):** 800-1200 words. Take a position and defend it. Not balanced "on the one hand, on the other hand" content. falcani has opinions; let them show. Example: "Why we tell some clients not to build custom software."

**Technical deep dives (Pillar 3):** 2000-3000 words. Architecture decisions, technology choices, performance optimization stories. Written so that Marcus-level readers get the depth they need but Linda-level readers can follow the first few paragraphs.

**SEO priorities (initial keyword targets):**
- "custom software for small business"
- "ERP alternatives for SMEs"
- "cost of manual processes"
- "spreadsheet to software migration"
- "custom software vs SaaS"
- "software ROI calculator"
- "[industry] process automation" (various industries, since falcani doesn't filter by industry)

### Newsletter: The Resolver

**Role:** Direct relationship with subscribers. The most personal, opinionated, and valuable content falcani produces. This is where people who already trust falcani go deeper.
**Audience:** Mix of all personas, but skewing toward people who've already had at least one touchpoint with falcani.
**Language:** Bilingual option. Let subscribers choose their preferred language at signup. Don't auto-translate; write natively in each.

**Cadence:** Weekly, Tuesdays at 10am (subscriber's local time if possible, otherwise EST/COT).

**Format:**

Each issue follows a loose structure that varies enough to stay unpredictable:

**The Opener (required):** A short, personal observation from a solver or the founder. Two to four sentences. Something that happened that week, something that made the team think. Not a summary of the newsletter. An actual thought.

**The Main Course (required):** One substantial piece of content. Could be a condensed blog post, a behind-the-scenes story, a technical breakdown, an uncomfortable truth, or a Pillar 9 origin piece. 400-800 words. This is the reason people open the email.

**The #falcanized Moment (when available):** A client win, a verb sighting, a Pateti-style story. Short, celebratory. Not every issue needs this; only include when it's genuine.

**The Link (required):** One link to the best thing falcani published that week (blog post, Instagram Reel, case study). Not three links, not five. One. Respect the reader's attention.

**The Close (required):** A question. Not rhetorical. Something that invites a reply. "What's the process your team dreads most?" "What would you fix first if time and budget weren't a constraint?" This turns the newsletter from broadcast into conversation.

**What The Resolver is not:**
- A roundup of falcani's social media posts
- A product update digest
- A promotional vehicle disguised as content
- A wall of links with no editorial voice

## 4. Cian: Deployment Rules

The brand guide defines who Cian is. This section defines how she's used across content.

### Where Cian Appears

**Instagram Reels (primary deployment):** Cian is the presenter for educational and explainer content. She faces the camera, explains a concept, walks through a before/after, or reacts to an industry problem. Her presence creates visual consistency across the feed: when someone sees Cian, they know it's a falcani explainer.

**Instagram Carousels:** Cian can appear as a visual guide within carousel slides, pointing at key information, reacting to data points, or illustrating concepts. She's not in every carousel; she appears when the content benefits from a "someone is showing you this" framing.

**Blog post illustrations:** Cian can appear as a recurring visual element in blog headers or within the post, particularly for tutorial and educational content. She signals "this is an accessible explanation" versus a pure technical deep dive.

**Newsletter:** Cian does not appear in The Resolver. The newsletter is personal and text-first. Adding an avatar would undermine the direct human-to-human feel.

**LinkedIn:** Cian does not appear on LinkedIn. The platform's audience expects real people and professional credibility. A stylized avatar on LinkedIn risks undermining trust with Marcus and Sophie personas.

### Where Cian Never Appears

- Proposals, contracts, or formal client documents
- Crisis communications
- LinkedIn (any format)
- The Resolver newsletter
- Legal or compliance materials
- Email signatures

### Cian's Content Rules

**She never sells.** Cian educates, explains, demonstrates, and celebrates. She never says "contact us," "book a demo," or "sign up now." Any CTA associated with Cian content comes from the caption or a separate visual element, not from Cian herself.

**She speaks in the brand voice, not her own.** Cian does not have a separate personality from falcani. She is the brand's personality made visible. She doesn't have catchphrases, personal opinions that differ from falcani's position, or running jokes that exist outside the brand.

**She switches languages naturally.** In a single Reel, Cian might start in English and switch to Spanish for emphasis, or vice versa. This should feel like code-switching, not like a translation service. The switch happens when the other language says it better.

**She's always stylized.** Never render Cian in a photorealistic style, even if the technology improves. The stylized treatment is a brand decision, not a technical limitation. If she ever looks "too real," that's a violation.

### Production Specifications (for AI generation and 3D rendering)

**Static images:** Minimum resolution 1080x1080px for Instagram, 1200x628px for blog headers. Always on Deep Navy or Clean White background. Outfit font for any text overlays within the image.

**Video/Reels:** 1080x1920px (9:16 vertical) for Instagram. 30fps minimum. Lip-sync required when Cian speaks. Voice should be natural-paced, bilingual-capable, conversational. No robotic TTS. If AI voice generation is used, it must pass a human-sounding quality check.

**Consistency across pieces:** Cian's appearance (hair, features, clothing style, color palette) must remain consistent across all content. Create a reference sheet with 5-8 angles and expressions that all future generations match against. Variation in outfit details is acceptable; variation in facial features or body proportions is not.

## 6. Editorial Calendar Framework

### Monthly Planning Structure

Each month has a theme drawn from the pillars. The theme doesn't restrict content; it provides a center of gravity.

**Month 1 (Launch): Establishing the Voice**
Focus pillars: 4 (Anti-Corporate), 9 (Origin), 7 (Education)
Goal: Make falcani's social presence feel distinct from every other tech company. Lead with personality, not services.

**Month 2: Proving the Work**
Focus pillars: 2 (Before/After), 1 (Cost of Doing Nothing), 3 (How We Build)
Goal: Shift from "who we are" to "what we've done." Show results.

**Month 3: Building Community**
Focus pillars: 5 (Small Business Champions), 6 (Solver Life), 8 (#falcanize Moment)
Goal: Make the audience feel like they're part of something. Celebrate clients, show culture, invite the verb.

**After Month 3:** Rotate freely based on performance data. Double down on what's generating engagement and leads.

### Weekly Rhythm

| Day | Instagram | LinkedIn | Blog | Newsletter |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Monday | Reel (educational or transformation) | | | |
| Tuesday | | Long-form text post | | The Resolver sends |
| Wednesday | Carousel or static | | | |
| Thursday | | Technical insight or story | Blog post (bi-weekly) | |
| Friday | Story series (BTS, solver life) | | | |
| Weekend | Optional: light content, repurposed highlights | | | |

This is a framework, not a prison. If something newsworthy happens on a Wednesday, post it. If a Reel does well on Monday, don't wait until the following Monday to post another one.

### Content Production Workflow

**Week planning (Friday for the following week):**
1. Review previous week's performance (engagement, clicks, DMs, form submissions)
2. Select 3-4 content pieces from the pillar backlog
3. Assign to format and channel
4. Draft or outline each piece
5. Schedule production (who creates what by when)

**Creation rules:**
- Every piece runs through the Anti-AI-Detection acid test before publishing
- Every piece identifies its primary persona (Linda, Marcus, or Sophie)
- Every piece ties to at least one content pillar
- No piece publishes without at least one specific detail (a number, a name, a concrete scenario)

## 7. SEO Strategy

### Approach

falcani's SEO strategy targets the questions Marcus, Linda, and Sophie are typing into Google before they know falcani exists. The goal is to show up when they're problem-aware but not solution-aware.

### Keyword Categories

**Problem-aware queries (highest priority):**
- "cost of manual data entry"
- "how much time does manual reporting waste"
- "spreadsheet problems growing business"
- "when to stop using Excel for operations"
- "signs your business has outgrown its tools"

**Solution-aware queries:**
- "custom software for small business"
- "custom software vs SaaS for SMEs"
- "ERP alternatives mid-size companies"
- "should I build custom software or buy SaaS"
- "custom software development cost"

**Technical queries (Marcus magnet):**
- "how to migrate from spreadsheets to custom software"
- "custom dashboard development"
- "legacy system modernization SME"
- "API integration for small business"
- "modular architecture for growing companies"

**ROI queries (Sophie magnet):**
- "software ROI calculator"
- "total cost of ownership custom software"
- "how to justify custom software investment"
- "custom software payback period"

### On-Page SEO Rules

Every blog post must include: a title tag under 60 characters that includes the primary keyword, a meta description under 155 characters that sounds like falcani (not like an SEO tool wrote it), at least one H2 that matches a common search query variation, internal links to related posts, and an external link to a credible source when it adds value.

**What falcani SEO is not:** Keyword-stuffed content that reads like it was written for a search engine. If optimizing a post makes it sound less like falcani, the optimization is wrong. Voice always wins over search ranking.

## 8. Content Repurposing Workflows

Every substantial piece of content should generate multiple derivative pieces across channels. This maximizes effort and ensures consistent messaging.

### Blog Post > Multi-Channel Workflow

1. **Blog post published** (1500-2500 words, Pillar 7 tutorial)
2. **LinkedIn condensed version** (same week): 600 words, key insight only, ends with a question. Links to full post.
3. **Instagram carousel** (same or following week): 5-7 slides extracting the most visual or surprising points. Cian appears on slide 1 as the "guide."
4. **Instagram Reel** (following week): Cian explains the core concept in 60 seconds. Different angle than the carousel; complementary, not repetitive.
5. **Newsletter inclusion** (following Tuesday): The post becomes "The Link" section with a 2-sentence teaser.

### Case Study > Multi-Channel Workflow

1. **Case study published on blog** (1000-1500 words)
2. **LinkedIn narrative** (same week): The story told in first person by the solver who led the project. Focus on one surprising moment. No bullet points.
3. **Instagram before/after** (same week): Visual comparison. Key number as the hero text overlay. "#falcanized" in caption.
4. **Newsletter feature** (following Tuesday): Condensed version as "The Main Course" with a personal angle.

### Uncomfortable Truth > Multi-Channel Workflow

1. **LinkedIn long-form post** (the provocative take, 800-1200 words)
2. **Instagram Reel** (same week): Cian delivers the core argument in 30-45 seconds. Direct, opinionated, no hedging.
3. **Blog expansion** (following week, if the LinkedIn post generates strong engagement): Full exploration of the topic with supporting evidence, examples, and nuance.
4. **Newsletter exploration** (following Tuesday): Deeper version with the responses and reactions the LinkedIn post generated.

## 9. Measurement & KPIs

### What We Measure (and What We Don't)

**We measure:**
- Inbound conversations: DMs, form submissions on falcani.com and ineedasoftwarefor.com, email replies to The Resolver. This is the primary metric. Content exists to start conversations.
- Engagement rate per post: Likes, comments, shares, saves. Compared to falcani's own baseline, not to industry averages.
- Website traffic from content: Blog sessions, referral traffic from social, newsletter click-through rate.
- Newsletter growth: Subscriber count, open rate, reply rate. Reply rate matters more than open rate.
- #falcanize usage: Organic mentions of the verb by people who aren't falcani. Track manually; there's no tool that does this well.

**We don't measure (or don't obsess over):**
- Follower count: Vanity metric. 500 engaged followers who DM questions are worth more than 50,000 passive followers.
- Impressions: Seeing a post is not the same as caring about it.
- "Brand awareness" as an abstract concept: If awareness doesn't convert to conversations, it's not working.

### Monthly Review Framework

First Monday of each month, review:

1. How many inbound conversations did content generate? (Target: set after 3 months of baseline data)
2. Which content pillars drove the most engagement? (Double down)
3. Which content pillars underperformed? (Diagnose why before cutting)
4. Did any post go viral or get unexpected reach? (Understand what happened and whether it's replicable)
5. Are we passing the acid test? (Read the last month's content critically. Does it sound like falcani or like a content machine?)
6. What did the audience ask or say? (Comments, DMs, and replies are market research)

## 10. Growth Tactics

### Instagram Growth

**Weeks 1-4: Foundation**
- Post daily (Reels prioritized, algorithm favors new accounts posting Reels)
- Follow and engage with 10-15 accounts per day in the SME space (not competitors; potential clients and adjacent communities)
- Respond to every comment within 2 hours
- Use location tags for Zipaquirá, Bogotá, and key client cities
- Hashtag strategy: 3-5 targeted hashtags per post, not 30. Focus on: #smallbusinesssoftware, #customsoftware, #smetech, #falcanize, #solversnotmanagers

**Weeks 5-12: Momentum**
- Introduce Cian as a regular presence in Reels
- Start cross-promoting newsletter in Stories
- Collaborate with one SME-focused account per month (not paid influencers; genuine businesses in the community)
- Share user-generated content when clients mention falcani or #falcanize

**Months 4+: Maturation**
- Evaluate whether Instagram is driving conversations (if not, diagnose content quality vs. audience fit)
- Consider Instagram Ads only if organic content is performing well (don't pay to amplify content that doesn't work organically)

### LinkedIn Growth

**Core tactics:**
- The founder posts from his personal account 1-2x/week with the company page tagged. Personal accounts get 5-10x the reach of company pages on LinkedIn. The founder's personal brand amplifies falcani's company page, not replaces it. Both should be active.
- Engage in comments on posts by SME leaders, operations professionals, and finance decision-makers. Add value, don't pitch.
- Connect with every person who engages with falcani content. Personal connection request from a real person, not a bot.
- Join and participate in 2-3 LinkedIn groups where SME decision-makers gather.

### Newsletter Growth

- Add newsletter signup to every blog post (inline, not just sidebar)
- Add newsletter CTA to Instagram bio link page
- Mention The Resolver in LinkedIn posts when the content is relevant ("I wrote more about this in this week's Resolver")
- Offer a valuable lead magnet for email capture: "The Cost of Manual Processes Calculator" (interactive tool on ineedasoftwarefor.com)

## 11. Domain Strategy

falcani operates three domains, each with a distinct purpose.

### falcani.com (Main Site)

**Purpose:** Primary brand presence. Portfolio, case studies, blog, about page, contact.
**Content role:** Home for the blog, case studies, and all evergreen content. The domain that appears in search results.
**SEO:** All blog content lives here. This domain builds domain authority over time.

### ineedasoftwarefor.com (Lead Generation Microsite)

**Purpose:** Conversion-focused entry point for problem-aware visitors who haven't heard of falcani yet.
**Content role:** The Software Needs Navigator form lives here. This is where people describe their pain and falcani starts the conversation.
**Strategy:** This domain should be the destination for ads, lead magnets, and cold outreach. Its URL tells the visitor exactly what to expect. Content on social media that targets problem-aware audiences ("is your team drowning in spreadsheets?") should link here, not to falcani.com.
**SEO:** Target problem-aware keywords. "I need a software for [specific use case]" long-tail queries.

### falcani.software (Instagram Companion)

**Purpose:** Vanity domain matching the Instagram handle @falcani.software. Brand consistency on social platforms.
**Content role:** 301 redirect to falcani.com. No hosted content, no hub page, no maintenance. The domain exists solely to match the Instagram handle and send anyone who types it to the main site.
**Strategy:** If a multi-link page is ever needed for Instagram bio purposes, build it at falcani.com/links rather than creating a separate experience on this domain. One fewer thing to maintain, one fewer place for the brand to drift.

## 12. What Success Looks Like

### 90 Days

- Instagram: 500+ followers with consistent engagement (comments, DMs, saves)
- LinkedIn: Post format aligned with brand guide, engagement rate increasing month over month
- Blog: 4+ new posts published, at least one ranking on page 1 for a target keyword
- Newsletter: 100+ subscribers, 40%+ open rate, at least 5 replies per issue
- Inbound: At least 3 qualified conversations that originated from content

### 6 Months

- Instagram: 2,000+ followers, Cian recognized as a visual anchor, at least 1 post per month driving a DM conversation
- LinkedIn: Founder's personal account and company page both active, combined reach growing
- Blog: 10+ posts, organic traffic growing month over month, at least 3 posts ranking page 1
- Newsletter: 500+ subscribers, reply rate above 5%, recognized as "something I actually read" by subscribers
- Inbound: Content is a consistent source of qualified leads (target: 2-3 per month minimum)
- #falcanize: At least 5 organic uses of the verb by non-falcani people

### 12 Months

- Content is falcani's primary lead generation channel
- The brand guide and this strategy are being consumed by AI tools that generate on-brand content with minimal human editing
