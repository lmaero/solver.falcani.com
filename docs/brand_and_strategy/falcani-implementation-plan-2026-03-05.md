# falcani: Brand Pivot Implementation Plan

> This is the action document. The brand guidelines define who falcani is. The content strategy defines what falcani publishes. This document defines what needs to happen, in what order, to make the first two documents real. Every task here is specific, sequenced, and temporary. Once completed, this document becomes an archive.

---

## How to Use This Document

Tasks are grouped into phases. Phases are sequential but tasks within a phase can run in parallel when they have no dependency on each other. Each task includes what needs to happen, why it matters, what "done" looks like, and any dependencies on other tasks.

Priority labels: **Critical** (blocks other work or damages the brand every day it remains undone), **High** (significant impact, do soon), **Medium** (important but not blocking), **Low** (nice to have, do when bandwidth allows).

### Task 1.6: Create Anti-AI-Detection Pre-Publish Checklist
**Priority:** High
**Current state:** The brand guide defines the Anti-AI-Detection acid test in detail, but there is no operationalized tool or template for efficiently running it on every piece of content before publication.
**Action:** Turn the brand guide's Anti-AI-Detection section into a reusable pre-publish checklist that any content creator (human or AI) can run in under 3 minutes. Include:
- Banned word scan (full list from brand guide: leverage, utilize, innovative, cutting-edge, seamless, robust, etc.)
- Em dash check (zero tolerance)
- Parallel structure audit (no more than two consecutive sentences with the same structure)
- Opening sentence check (does it start with a specific detail, not a generic observation?)
- "Logo swap" test (could this appear on any other tech company's page? If yes, rewrite)
- Persona alignment check (who is this for: Linda, Marcus, or Sophie?)
- Content pillar check (which pillar does this tie to?)
- Specific detail requirement (does it contain at least one number, name, or concrete scenario?)

Format options: a simple markdown checklist, a Notion template, or a lightweight script that scans text for banned words and em dashes.
**Done when:** Checklist exists, is accessible to everyone who publishes content, and has been tested on at least 3 pieces of content.
**Dependencies:** None.
**Owner:** Luis or content operations solver.

## Phase 2: Content Production Kickoff (Days 15-30)

Infrastructure is in place. Start producing content according to the strategy.

### Task 2.1: Produce First 5 Blog Posts
**Priority:** Critical
**Current state:** Blog is live but empty. The content strategy requires 2 posts/month minimum, but the launch period needs a stronger foundation.
**Action:** Write and publish 5 blog posts to seed the blog with enough content to demonstrate depth. Suggested first batch (aligned with Month 1 editorial focus on Pillars 4, 9, 7):
1. **Origin story post** (Pillar 9): "Why an aeronautical engineer started a software company." The Zipaquirá walk, the family name, the decision. 1000-1500 words.
2. **Opinion post** (Pillar 4): "Stop paying for PowerPoints about software." Anti-corporate take on the consulting industry. 800-1200 words.
3. **Tutorial post** (Pillar 7): "When to stop using Excel for your operations (and what to do instead)." Targets the SEO keyword "when to stop using Excel for operations." 1500-2500 words.
4. **Case study** (Pillar 2): A real falcani project, told with specific numbers, timelines, and outcomes. 1000-1500 words. Requires client permission.
5. **Uncomfortable truth** (Pillar 10): "Why we sometimes tell clients not to build custom software." Honest take that builds trust. 800-1200 words.

**Every post must:**
- Pass the Anti-AI-Detection acid test
- Include SEO elements (title tag, meta description, target keyword)
- Include newsletter signup CTA
- Include at least one specific detail (number, name, scenario)
- Identify its primary persona
**Done when:** All 5 posts are live, indexed by Google, and linked from appropriate social posts.
**Dependencies:** Task 1.2 (blog is set up). Task 1.6 (pre-publish checklist exists for acid testing every post).
**Owner:** Luis + content solver (can use AI tools for drafts, but every draft must be humanized per the Anti-AI-Detection rules).

## Phase 3: System Activation (Days 31-60)

Content is flowing. Now activate the systems that make it sustainable.

### Task 3.1: Implement the Weekly Content Production Workflow
**Priority:** High
**Action:** Establish the weekly rhythm defined in the content strategy:
- **Friday planning session:** Review previous week's performance, select 3-4 content pieces from the pillar backlog, assign to format and channel, draft or outline, schedule production.
- **Creation rules enforced for every piece:** Anti-AI-Detection acid test, primary persona identified, content pillar tied, at least one specific detail.
- **Weekly output targets:** 3-4 Instagram posts, 2-3 LinkedIn posts, newsletter every Tuesday, blog post bi-weekly.
**Done when:** The workflow has been followed for 3 consecutive weeks with consistent output.
**Dependencies:** Phases 1 and 2 complete.
**Owner:** Luis + content team (even if the "team" is just Luis and AI tools for now).

### Task 3.2: Execute Content Repurposing Workflows
**Priority:** Medium
**Action:** Take the first 2 blog posts and run them through the full repurposing workflow:
- Blog post > LinkedIn condensed version (same week)
- Blog post > Instagram carousel (same or following week)
- Blog post > Instagram Reel with Cian (following week)
- Blog post > Newsletter link inclusion (following Tuesday)
Track how long each repurposing step takes to calibrate future production capacity.
**Done when:** 2 blog posts have been fully repurposed across all 4 channels per the workflow.
**Dependencies:** Task 2.1 (blog posts exist), Task 2.5 (Cian content production pipeline is functional).
**Owner:** Content creator.

### Task 3.3: Set Up Measurement Dashboard
**Priority:** Medium
**Action:** Create a simple tracking system for the KPIs defined in the content strategy:
- Inbound conversations (DMs, form submissions, email replies) per week
- Engagement rate per post per channel
- Website traffic from content (blog sessions, social referrals, newsletter clicks)
- Newsletter metrics (subscriber count, open rate, reply rate)
- #falcanize organic usage (manual tracking)

This doesn't need to be a fancy tool. A spreadsheet works. What matters is that the data is collected consistently so the monthly review (Task 3.4) has something to work with.
**Done when:** Dashboard exists and is being populated weekly.
**Dependencies:** Content is being published (Phase 2 tasks).
**Owner:** Luis.

### Task 3.4: Conduct First Monthly Content Review
**Priority:** High
**Action:** First Monday after 30 days of content production, run the monthly review framework:
1. How many inbound conversations did content generate?
2. Which content pillars drove the most engagement?
3. Which pillars underperformed? (Diagnose before cutting.)
4. Did any post get unexpected reach? (Understand why.)
5. Are we passing the acid test? (Read the last month's content critically.)
6. What did the audience ask or say?

Use the findings to adjust the Month 2 editorial plan (shift to Pillars 2, 1, 3 per the calendar framework, adjusted by performance data).
**Done when:** Review is documented, Month 2 plan is drafted with data-informed adjustments.
**Dependencies:** 30 days of content production complete.
**Owner:** Luis.

## Phase 5: Ongoing Maintenance (Continuous)

These are not one-time tasks. They run as long as falcani produces content.

### Task 5.1: Anti-AI-Detection Acid Test on Every Piece of Content
**Frequency:** Before every publish.
**Action:** Run every piece of content through the Anti-AI-Detection checklist from the brand guide before it goes live. Check for: em dashes, banned words (leverage, utilize, innovative, cutting-edge, etc.), parallel structure abuse, formulaic openings, corporate buzzwords, any sentence that sounds like a language model wrote it.
**Owner:** Whoever publishes the content.

### Task 5.2: Monthly Content Review
**Frequency:** First Monday of every month.
**Action:** Run the monthly review framework (same as Task 3.4) and adjust the next month's editorial plan accordingly.
**Owner:** Luis.

### Task 5.3: Quarterly Brand Guide and Strategy Audit
**Frequency:** Every 3 months.
**Action:** Re-read the brand guidelines and content strategy in full. Ask:
- Is anything outdated?
- Has the brand evolved in a way that the documents haven't captured?
- Are there new content pillars that should be added?
- Has the audience map shifted?
- Does Cian's deployment need adjustment based on audience reception?

Update the documents if needed. These are living documents, not artifacts.
**Owner:** Luis.

### Task 5.4: Cian Consistency Check
**Frequency:** Monthly.
**Action:** Compare all Cian content produced that month against the reference sheet. Flag any visual drift (face changes, proportion shifts, style inconsistencies). Correct before the next batch of Cian content.
**Owner:** Content creator.

### Task 5.5: #falcanize Organic Tracking
**Frequency:** Weekly.
**Action:** Search for organic mentions of #falcanize, "falcanized," and variations. Record who used it, in what context, and whether it was prompted by falcani content or truly organic. This is the strongest signal of brand penetration.
**Owner:** Luis or community manager.

## What This Plan Does Not Cover

This plan assumes:
- **Pricing and proposals** are handled separately. The brand guide provides financial framing principles, but specific pricing decisions are out of scope.
- **Hiring solvers** is a separate process. If content production demands exceed Luis's bandwidth, hiring or contracting help is a business decision, not a brand task.
- **Paid advertising creative** is deferred until organic content proves itself (Task 4.4). If paid ads become relevant, they need their own brief.
- **Product development** on falcani.com or ineedasoftwarefor.com beyond brand compliance is out of scope. Feature decisions for the Software Needs Navigator, client portal, or other tools are product decisions.
- **Sonic identity production** (creating actual brand music/audio) requires a separate creative brief with a musician or audio producer. The brand guide defines the sonic profile (tight modern rock, 110-140 BPM, 70% riff-driven/20% electronic edge/10% Latin rhythmic accent); translating that into actual audio assets is a production task that needs its own timeline and budget.
